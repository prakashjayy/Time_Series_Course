What is a Time-Series ?¶

A time series is a series of data points indexed (or listed or graphed) in time order. Most commonly, a time series is a sequence taken at successive equally spaced points in time. Thus it is a sequence of discrete-time data.

What is Time-Series Forecasting? (Predictive Analytics)¶

Time series forecasting is the use of a model to predict future values based on previously observed values.
Example: Power demand of a city in the next 10 series.

What is Time-series Analysis? (Descriptive Analytics)¶

Time series analysis comprises methods for analyzing time series data in order to extract meaningful statistics and other characteristics of the data.
Example: Effect of sales of a restaurant due to demontisation.

What can be Forecasted?¶

A few examples include:

  • Building power generation plat in the next 5 years: Requires forecasts of future demand
  • Scheduling staff in a call center next week requires forecasts of call volumes
  • Stocking an inventory requires forecasts of stock requirements.

Factors to consider while Forecasting?¶

  • How much data is Available?
  • How well we understand the factors that contribute to it?
  • Weather the forecast can effect the things we are trying to forecast?

Different kinds of forecasting?¶

  • Short-term Forecasts

    • scheduling of personal.
    • production and transportation.

  • Medium-term forecasts

    • Determine future resource requirements.

  • Long-term forecasts

    • Strategic planning of a company.

Forecast Horizons¶

  • Will forecasts be required for next day, next week, next 6 months, next 10 years?
  • How frequently are forecasts needed?
  • What kind of forecasts are needed?

Data Collections¶

Insights - Time-Plot:¶

  • There is a clear and increasing trend.
  • There is also a strong seasonal pattern that increases in size as the level of the series increases.
  • The sudden drop at the start of each year is caused by a government subsidisation scheme that makes it cost-effective for patients to stockpile drugs at the end of the calendar year.

Any forecasts of this series would need to capture the seasonal pattern, and the fact that the trend is changing slowly.

Insights-Seasonal Plot¶

  • There is a large jump in sales in January each year.
  • The graph also shows that there was an unusually small number of sales in March 2008 (most other years show an increase between February and March).
  • The small number of sales in June 2008 is probably due to incomplete counting of sales at the time the data were collected.

from pandas.tools.plotting import lag_plot
plot_lags = 25
rows = int(plot_lags/5)
cols = int(plot_lags/5)
fig, axes = plt.subplots(rows, cols, sharex=True, sharey=True)
fig.set_figwidth(plot_lags)
fig.set_figheight(plot_lags)
count =1
for i in range(rows):
    for j in range(cols):
        lag_plot(ts["y"], lag=count, ax=axes[i, j])
        count+=1
